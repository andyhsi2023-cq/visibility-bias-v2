Dear Editor,

I am submitting the enclosed manuscript, “Measuring Visibility Bias in Bureaucratic Text: A Validated Instrument with Evidence from Chinese Municipal Government Work Reports”, for consideration as a Research Article at Journal of Computational Social Science.

The manuscript introduces the Visibility Attention Index (VAI) — a pre-registered text-measurement instrument for compositional policy attention in Chinese municipal government work reports (6,294 documents, 282 prefectures, 2002–2024) — and subjects it to a five-test construct-validity battery in the Cronbach-Meehl tradition.

The paper’s primary methodological contribution is a within-document retrospective-vs-prospective behavioral signature: in 4,330 reports with cleanly-separable past-year review and next-year plan sections, the retrospective section is systematically more visibility-loaded than the prospective section (Δ = +0.025, paired t = 8.4, p < 10⁻¹⁶). Same lexicon, same author, same report — so the differential is not confounded by lexicon-selection bias, author-style effects, or report-length artifacts. To my knowledge, this kind of within-document behavioral validation has not been previously exploited in the text-as-data literature and is portable to any corpus of bureaucratic documents with temporal section structure (e.g., EU National Reform Programmes, Indian state budget speeches, Mexican municipal Plan de Desarrollo documents).

Three additional features position the paper for JCSS’s readership:

  1. Explicit domain-scope documentation. A pre-registered third-party validation against Chinese Wikipedia city descriptions returns a null (r = −0.15), which I interpret as scoping the instrument to governance-rhetoric rather than encyclopedic-description domain. Explicit scope boundaries of this kind are rarely reported in applied text-measurement work and align with JCSS’s emphasis on transparent computational social-science research.

  2. Full pre-registration and null-reporting discipline. OSF DOI 10.17605/OSF.IO/ZMJY5 (archived 2026-04-14). Two pre-registered null results are reported transparently, including one (an event study around central anti-corruption inspections) whose narrow-window TWFE finding does not survive heterogeneity-robust estimation under an extended sample. I report the sign reversal prominently rather than bury it.

  3. Complete replication archive. All construction code, lexicons, all five validity-test scripts, all five S7 audit reports, and the deviation log are archived at Zenodo DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19569979 and mirrored at GitHub (andyhsi2023-cq/visibility-bias-v2).
