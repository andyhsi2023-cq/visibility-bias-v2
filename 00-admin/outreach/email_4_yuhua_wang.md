Dear Professor Wang,

I am writing to share a text-measurement project on Chinese municipal governance and to request your reaction, given your published work on measurement-focused approaches to Chinese politics.

Your work on bureaucratic control mechanisms and institutional measurement in Chinese history has been an important reference point. My current project takes a narrower but methodologically-focused angle: I construct a Visibility Attention Index (VAI) from 6,294 prefecture-level government work reports (2002–2024) to measure the compositional share of *visible* versus *functional* policy attention in urban-renewal discourse.

The paper is structured as a methodological contribution with a five-test pre-registered construct-validity battery (OSF DOI 10.17605/OSF.IO/ZMJY5). Four tests pass, including a novel within-document retrospective-vs-prospective differential (Δ = +0.025, paired t = 8.4, p < 10⁻¹⁶) that I believe has not been previously exploited in the text-as-data literature. The fifth test — a pre-registered validation against Chinese Wikipedia city descriptions — returns a null (r = −0.15), which I interpret as a scope finding (governance-rhetoric vs encyclopedic description).

I would value your view particularly on: (i) whether the construct-validity protocol meets the bar for a measurement instrument in Chinese-politics research; (ii) whether the domain-scope claim (governance-rhetoric, full stop) is the right way to handle the Wikipedia null.

I would welcome your engagement at whatever level works — substantive feedback, acknowledgment, or co-authorship on the measurement-focused paper. I am finalizing the manuscript (~9,500 words) and am considering methodology venues (Political Analysis; Research & Politics; Journal of Computational Social Science).
